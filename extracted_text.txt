Program Overview
What Is the Alumni Mentorship Program?
Our Mission

Launched in 2021, this initiative pairs distinguished E&E alumni with small groups of current students. Mentors share their expertise, real-world experience and professional networks to help students navigate academic choices and career challenges with confidence.

Industry Alumni

Working professionals guiding students on career paths

Academia Alumni

Researchers and professors supporting higher studies

Entrepreneur Alumni

Founders inspiring innovation and startups

The Role of a Mentor
Guides, Advisors & Champions
🎯 Guide & Advisor

Mentors identify student strengths and co-create personalized pathways to bring out the best in every mentee.

🧠 Adaptive Learning

Recognizing that every student learns differently, mentors experiment with approaches suited to each individual.

💪 Build Confidence

Mentors help students break through mental blocks and develop the resilience needed for upcoming career challenges.

How It Works
Mentorship in Practice
Semester Report
Ongoing Sessions
Virtual Launch
Pairing

The program is designed to be flexible and student-driven. After the official virtual launch, student groups coordinate directly with their mentors to schedule regular online sessions — typically once or twice a month — based on mutual availability.

Program Structure
How Students & Mentors Connect
Small Groups, Big Impact

Each mentor works with 4–5 students, ensuring personalized attention and meaningful engagement.

Virtual & Flexible

All interactions happen online, making it easy for alumni across geographies to participate.

Student-Led Coordination

Student groups take ownership of scheduling and follow-ups, building leadership skills along the way.

Student Outcomes
What Students Gain
Technical Knowledge

Mentors help students stay current with industry trends and deepen their technical expertise beyond the syllabus.

Career Guidance

Informed decisions on electives, internships, and projects aligned with real-world market demands.

Professional Network

Students build valuable technical contacts that can open doors to opportunities throughout their careers.
